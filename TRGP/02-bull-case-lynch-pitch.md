# TRGP Bull Case: The Lynch Pitch
## Why Would I Own This Stock?

**Date:** March 24, 2026
**Source:** FY2025 10-K (filed February 19, 2026), Finnhub, Alpha Vantage

---

### 1. What does the company actually do?

Targa Resources gathers raw natural gas from oil and gas wells (primarily in the Permian Basin), processes it to extract valuable NGLs (natural gas liquids), then transports those NGLs via its own pipeline system to Mont Belvieu, Texas, where it fractionates them into individual products (ethane, propane, butane, natural gasoline) and exports LPG from its Galena Park Marine Terminal.

In simple terms: Targa is a toll road for natural gas liquids, connecting the most prolific oil basin in the world (the Permian) to the largest NGL market hub (Mont Belvieu) and the global export market.

### 2. What is the simple reason this stock could work?

The Permian Basin keeps growing. As long as oil prices support drilling, associated natural gas production grows, and all that gas needs to be gathered, processed, and its NGLs fractionated. Targa has the infrastructure in place -- and is building more -- to capture that growth.

From the 2025 10-K: Total Permian plant natural gas inlet grew 11% year-over-year to 6,391 MMcf/d. Permian NGL production grew 12% to 880.6 MBbl/d. Targa brought online THREE new 275 MMcf/d processing plants in 2025 alone (Bull Moose, Pembrook II, Bull Moose II) and has SEVEN more in various stages of construction through 2027.

This is a good business because:
- 8% volume growth across the entire system in 2025
- Adjusted EBITDA grew 20% to $4,957.4 million (from $4,142.3 million in 2024)
- Net income attributable to shareholders grew 45% to $1,852.5 million
- The integrated gathering-to-export value chain captures margin at every step

### 3. How does the company actually make money?

Two segments:
- **Gathering and Processing** (adjusted operating margin: $3,346.2M in 2025): Collects fees and commodity margins for gathering and processing natural gas. Permian volumes drive growth.
- **Logistics and Transportation** (adjusted operating margin: $3,182.0M in 2025): Transports mixed NGLs via pipeline to Mont Belvieu, fractionates into products, exports LPG. Pipeline transport volumes grew 21%, fractionation grew 13%.

Total revenues: $17.0 billion. Fee-based revenue is growing as a percentage of the mix.

### 4. What does the balance sheet look like today?

From the 2025 10-K (as of December 31, 2025):
- Cash on hand: $166.1 million (consolidated)
- Total liquidity as of January 31, 2026: $1,922.2 million
- TRGP Revolver capacity: $3.5 billion (with $500M accordion)
- Interest expense: $852.8 million (up 11% due to higher borrowings)
- Adjusted EBITDA: $4,957.4 million
- Growth capital expenditures: $3,343.5 million
- Maintenance capex: $226.4 million
- Adjusted free cash flow: $539.0 million (up from $140.1M in 2024)
- Dividend: $4.00/share ($1.67% yield at ~$237/share)

The balance sheet is leveraged but manageable for a midstream company of this scale. The revolver matures in 2030. Debt maturities are staggered across 2026-2055.

### 5. What kind of company is this?

**Fast grower transitioning to stalwart.** Targa is in the middle of a massive capital cycle -- 10 processing plants, 3 fractionation trains, the Speedway NGL pipeline, and LPG export expansion all under construction or recently completed. Once this build-out cycle matures (likely by 2028), the company should generate substantially more free cash flow as growth capex declines.

### 6. What could go wrong?

- Oil prices fall below $50/bbl, killing Permian drilling activity
- NGL oversupply compresses fractionation and marketing margins
- Construction cost overruns on the massive capital program (Speedway alone is a 500-mile, 30-inch pipeline)
- Interest rates stay elevated, increasing the cost of the heavy debt load
- Commodity price exposure on non-hedged/non-fee volumes

### 7. Am I too late or would I be missing this?

The stock has run significantly -- trading near $237 with a 52-week range of $142-$250. At 27.9x trailing earnings and 14.1x EV/EBITDA, it is not cheap. But the growth pipeline is real: 7+ plants under construction, Speedway pipeline, 3 new frac trains, LPG export expansion. If EBITDA grows to $5.5-6.0B by 2027 as these projects come online, the current valuation may be reasonable.

The analyst consensus is overwhelmingly bullish: 26 of 29 analysts rate it Buy or Strong Buy. Target price: $247.85.

### 8. Bottom line

Targa Resources is the best-positioned midstream operator to capture Permian Basin growth through an integrated gathering-processing-pipeline-fractionation-export system. The investment works if Permian production keeps growing and NGL demand (especially exports) remains strong. You would be wrong if oil prices collapse, NGL margins compress, or the $3.3B+ annual capital program encounters material cost overruns or delays.
